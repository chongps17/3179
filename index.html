Australian Bushfires

Introduction

Due to Australia's geographical location, bushfires have become a prominent issue for Australians and the environment. These bushfires not only put the lives of Australians at risk but cause severe damage to Australia's wildlife and natural landscapes. This visualisation aims to explore the geographical location of fires across Australia, the characteristics of these fires and how they affect aspects of our enviroment such as our forests and water quality.

Source: The New York Times

Fires detected across Australia

This map details the location of fires across Australia, with brightness indicating the temperature of the fire and Fire Radiative Power (FRP) indicating the intensity of the fire based on satellite signals. The time frame of fires shown on the map can be adjusted using the date brushing feature in the bar graph.

The overall map shows that the majority of fires happen near the coast of Australian states with a particular density in Queensland, New South Wales and the Northern Territory. The bar graph also shows that the highest number of fires country-wide is around the first half of September in the data set which may be a result of seasonal changes.
